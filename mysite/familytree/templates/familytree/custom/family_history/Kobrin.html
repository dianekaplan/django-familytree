Larry Kaplan's family:

The Kobrin side:

  • We have Russian/Lithuanian census records back to the 1700s for our 'Kabrun' ancestors: Nokhim Kabron (born in 1700's) had a son Khaim Kabron (1768-1822) who married Frume Kabron (1771- after 1834). They had a son Orel Kabron (1800-1831), who had a son Josel/Yosel/Iosel Kabron (1824-1877) who was a craftsman by trade. He had children Aron David, Esther/Etta-Mera, and Brayne/Brina. Aron David was born in Kovno, Russia (which became Kaunas, Lithuania), and he moved to Panevezys community in 1884).
  • In 1896, Aaron & Rochel Kobrin left Russia in (the area that's now Lithuania ) with their children, and settled in South Africa and the US around the same time. Kal Kobrin (Larry's father, 3 generations back) was 20 years old- settled in the US, and when he immigrated his surname was changed from Kobrin to Kaplan. Kalman's mother Rochel had come to the US in 1894, and then she went to South Africa afterward and died in Johannesburg. Six of Aaron & Rochel's children had families of their own:
    • Kalman's descendants include the Klopers, the Schultzes, the Almans, the Kaplans, the Dolphs, the Marcuses, and some Kobrins.
    • Nathan's descendants include the Rothschilds, the Joffees, the Goldsteins, and some Kobrins.
    • Annie's descendants are the Slonimskys, the Slons, and the Rosses.
    • Yetta's descendants are the Blochs and the Foxes
    • Solomon's descendants include the Melmans, the Robertsons, the Chaits, the Allans, and some Kobrins.
    • Beila's descendants include the Sneags, the Fittinghoffs, and the Markses

The Shapiro side:

  • Toba & Max Shapiro (4 generations back) lived in Lithuania and their daughter Annie was born there in 1876. They moved together to South Africa , and Annie came to the US sometime between 1876 and 1896. (Need to confirm whether her parents did too). Annie married Kalman Kobrin/Kaplan, and they raised Joe, Sarah, and Larry.
Background for both families:
  • Lithuania had been struggling with oppression under the Russian empire and famine in 1867-1868, and according to wikipedia between 1868 and 1914, approximately 635,000 people (almost 20 percent of the population) left Lithuania.
  • Toba & Max Shapiro moved from Russia to Johannesburg, South Africa. Their daughters Annie + Rebecca Shapiro (sisters) married Kalman and Solomon Kobrin (brothers). Annie & Kalman in the US (they were married in Boston June 15, 1896), and Rebecca & Solomon stayed in South Africa. There is still a large part of the family in South Africa, and over the years various folks have migrated to the US and the UK, and we have a couple families in Switzerland, Israel and Zimbabwe.
